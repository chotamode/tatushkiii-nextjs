# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective tattoo clients of SANDU, a tattoo artist based in Prague. Primary audience is Russian-speaking, with a meaningful share of English- and Czech-speaking visitors (the site is fully localized EN/CS/RU via a manual language switcher — no locale is treated as secondary). They land on the site from Instagram/Telegram or search, browse the portfolio to judge fit and style, and the job to be done is deciding to book a session and actually filling out the booking form (embedded OpnForm) or reaching out via Instagram/Telegram/WhatsApp.

## Product Purpose

A single-artist portfolio and booking site. It exists to turn interest (from social media, word of mouth, or search) into booked sessions. Success is a submitted booking form or a direct DM — everything else (about, process/FAQ, testimonials) exists to build enough confidence to get there.

## Brand Personality

Avant-garde, bold/edgy, minimalist. Voice and visuals lean cybersigilism/underground-art rather than salon-polished: black-and-white photography, sigil glyphs, glitch/grain texture, sharp geometric display type (Syne) contrasted with quiet monospace labels. Confident and a little confrontational, never soft or cute.

## Anti-references

Generic tattoo-shop clipart and stock template aesthetics: a saturated red used as a CTA/brand color (the default "tattoo parlor" move), stock ink-splatter or tribal-icon decoration, "shop catalog" card grids, anything that reads as a franchise template rather than one artist's own work. Also avoid corporate/SaaS landing-page grammar (gradient text, uniform icon+heading cards, eyebrow labels on every section) — the existing avant-garde/underground identity should stay the loudest voice, not be diluted toward safe-and-generic.

Note: a sparse ink-red accent (`--color-ink-red`), used only as small flecks — a glyph, a dot, a bracket, never a button's own color — is deliberate brand voice, not an anti-reference. It's drawn from the artist's own red-ink work, not from tattoo-parlor stock red.

## Design Principles

- **The work is the pitch.** Photography of real tattoos carries more weight than any copy or decoration — layout should showcase it large and grayscale-consistent, never compete with it.
- **Raw over polished-corporate.** Grain, glitch, asymmetric grids, and sigil motifs are deliberate brand texture, not rough edges to smooth over.
- **Every path leads to booking.** Nav, hero CTA, and contact section all point at the same booking form; new sections or features should reinforce that single conversion goal, not add competing ones.
- **No language is an afterthought.** RU is the primary audience but EN/CS get equal design treatment — no locale should visibly break layout, truncate, or read as a bolted-on translation.
- **Decorative ≠ interactive.** Flavor text and sigils should look inert; only real controls (booking button, actual filters) should carry hover/interactive affordances — a lesson learned from the redundant category-label buttons.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Baseline WCAG AA (contrast, keyboard operability, readable text) is sufficient. No specific additional accommodations requested.
